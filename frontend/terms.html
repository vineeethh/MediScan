2. Medical Disclaimer

Important: MediScan is NOT a substitute for professional medical care. All information provided by MediScan is for informational and educational purposes only.

MediScan's AI outputs — including symptom analyses, health guidance, medication information, lab report explanations, and diagnosis suggestions — are not medical advice. They do not constitute a diagnosis, treatment recommendation, or prescription.

You should always:

  • Consult a qualified medical professional (MBBS / MD / Specialist) before making any health decisions.
  • Not delay seeking medical care because of something you read or heard from MediScan.
  • Not disregard professional medical advice based on MediScan's output.
  • Verify any medication, dosage, or treatment information with a licensed pharmacist or doctor.

The accuracy of AI-generated health information is inherently limited. Medical knowledge evolves rapidly; the AI may not have the most current information.

3. MediScan is Not a Doctor

MediScan is powered by a large language model (Claude 3.5 Sonnet via OpenRouter) combined with a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline grounded in ICMR guidelines. While we have implemented medical guardrails and clinical interview protocols, the AI:

  • Does not have access to your complete medical history unless you share it in conversation.
  • Cannot physically examine you.
  • Cannot order laboratory tests.
  • Cannot issue prescriptions.
  • May make factual errors or have outdated information.
  • Does not replace a licensed MBBS / MD doctor in any capacity.
MediScan's clinical interview style is designed to help you articulate your symptoms more clearly — not to provide a final diagnosis. Always share the conversation summary with your actual doctor.

4. Emergency Information

MediScan is not designed for emergency use. If you are experiencing any of the following, call emergency services immediately:

  • Chest pain, pressure, or tightness
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Signs of stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
  • Severe bleeding or trauma
  • Loss of consciousness or unresponsiveness
  • Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
  • Suicidal thoughts or mental health crisis

6. Your Data & Privacy

Information We Collect

When you use MediScan, we may collect:

  • Conversation data: Messages you send and AI responses (stored in MongoDB for session continuity).
  • Uploaded files: PDF medical reports you upload for analysis (processed in memory and stored as extracted text only; original files are not retained after processing).
  • Account information: If you register via Firebase Authentication: your email address and display name.
  • Medication reminders: Stored locally in your browser's localStorage only — not transmitted to our servers.

How We Use Your Data

  • To provide AI health guidance during your session.
  • To improve the quality of the Service (in aggregate, anonymized form).
  • Conversation data may be used to provide session continuity.
Guest Mode MediScan does not require registration. Guests can use all core features without creating an account. Guest conversations are session-scoped and not linked to any identity.

Third-Party Services

MediScan uses the following third-party services, each with their own privacy policies:

  • OpenRouter / Anthropic Claude: Your messages are sent to the Claude API for AI processing.
  • Firebase (Google): Optional authentication and user management.
  • Qdrant Cloud: Vector database for RAG context retrieval (ICMR guidelines).
  • Overpass API / Nominatim (OpenStreetMap): Hospital location search — no personal data is shared.

7. AI Accuracy & Limitations

MediScan's AI responses are generated probabilistically and may:

  • Contain factual errors or outdated medical information.
  • Misinterpret symptoms, especially in complex or rare conditions.
  • Not account for local Indian disease patterns in all cases.
  • Produce different responses to the same input across sessions.

Our RAG pipeline attempts to ground responses in ICMR (Indian Council of Medical Research) guidelines, but this grounding is not guaranteed for every response. Always verify medical information with a qualified professional.

The Health Report Analyzer is designed for text-based PDFs only. Scanned images or handwritten reports cannot be accurately analyzed.
