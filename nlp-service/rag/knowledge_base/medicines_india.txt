COMMON MEDICINES IN INDIA — REFERENCE GUIDE

=== ANALGESICS AND ANTIPYRETICS ===
Paracetamol (Acetaminophen):
Brand names: Crocin, Dolo 650, Calpol, Tylenol (less common in India), Metacin, Fepanil
Uses: Fever, mild to moderate pain (headache, toothache, body ache), post-vaccination fever.
Dosage: Adults — 500mg-1000mg every 4-6 hours, maximum 4g/day. Children — 10-15 mg/kg every 4-6 hours.
Important: Do NOT use ibuprofen or aspirin for dengue fever (risk of bleeding). Paracetamol is safe for dengue.
Caution: Avoid alcohol. Liver toxicity with overdose or chronic alcohol use.

Ibuprofen:
Brand names: Brufen, Combiflam (ibuprofen + paracetamol), Advil (less common)
Uses: Fever, pain, inflammation — arthritis, menstrual cramps, sports injuries.
Avoid: In dengue, peptic ulcer, kidney disease, third trimester pregnancy. Take with food.

Diclofenac:
Brand names: Voveran, Voltaren, Dynapar
Uses: Acute pain, arthritis, musculoskeletal pain, dysmenorrhoea.
Available as: Tablets, injections, gel (topical).

=== ANTIBIOTICS ===
Amoxicillin/Amoxicillin-Clavulanate:
Brand names: Mox, Novamox, Augmentin, Amoxyclav
Uses: Throat infections, ear infections, sinusitis, pneumonia, UTI.
Dosage: 250-500mg three times daily for 5-10 days.
Important: Complete the full course. Do not stop when feeling better.

Azithromycin:
Brand names: Zithromax, Azee, Azithral
Uses: Respiratory infections, typhoid, chlamydia. Z-pack — 500mg day 1, then 250mg days 2-5.
Advantage: Once daily dosing, shorter course.

Ciprofloxacin:
Brand names: Ciplox, Cifran, Neofloxin
Uses: Typhoid, UTI, traveller's diarrhoea, respiratory infections.
Caution: Avoid in children and pregnancy. Can cause tendon damage.

Metronidazole:
Brand names: Flagyl, Metrogyl, Aristogyl
Uses: Amoebiasis, giardiasis, bacterial vaginosis, dental infections, anaerobic infections.
Dosage: 400mg three times daily for 5-7 days.
Avoid alcohol during treatment (severe reaction).

Doxycycline:
Brand names: Doxt-SL, Vibramycin, Doxybac
Uses: Malaria prophylaxis, cholera, rickettsial infections, acne.
Take with food and water. Avoid sun exposure (photosensitivity).

=== ANTACIDS AND GI MEDICINES ===
Pantoprazole/Omeprazole:
Brand names: Pan, Pantodac, Omez, Prilosec
Uses: Acidity, GERD, peptic ulcer, H. pylori eradication.
Take 30 minutes before meals. Usually safe for long-term use.

Ranitidine (largely withdrawn — replaced by Pantoprazole):
Earlier brand: Rantac, Zinetac.

Ondansetron:
Brand names: Emeset, Ondem, Zofran
Uses: Nausea and vomiting — chemotherapy, surgery, gastroenteritis.
Dosage: 4-8mg.

ORS (Oral Rehydration Salts):
Brand names: Electral, ORS-Wallis, Pedialyte, homemade (6 tsp sugar + 0.5 tsp salt per litre water)
Uses: Dehydration from diarrhoea, vomiting, heat exhaustion. Essential first-line treatment.
Free at all government health centres and anganwadis.

=== ANTIDIABETICS ===
Metformin:
Brand names: Glyciphage, Glucophage, Bigomet, Obimet
Uses: First-line drug for Type 2 diabetes. Also used in PCOS.
Dosage: 500mg-2000mg daily with meals.
Side effects: Nausea, diarrhoea (usually temporary). Avoid in kidney disease.

Glimepiride/Glipizide (Sulfonylureas):
Brand names: Amaryl, Glimisave, Glimpid
Uses: Type 2 diabetes, add-on to Metformin.
Risk of hypoglycaemia (low blood sugar) — always carry sugar/glucose.

Insulin:
Types: Rapid-acting (Novorapid, Humalog), Intermediate (NPH/Humulin N), Long-acting (Lantus/Glargine, Tresiba/Degludec).
Available at Jan Aushadhi stores at subsidised rates.

=== ANTIHYPERTENSIVES ===
Amlodipine:
Brand names: Amlip, Amlong, Stamlo, Norvasc
Uses: Hypertension, angina. Once daily. Safe in elderly.

Enalapril/Ramipril (ACE inhibitors):
Brand names: Envas, Cardace, Altace
Uses: Hypertension, heart failure, diabetic kidney protection.
Side effect: Dry cough — switch to ARB (Losartan) if cough occurs.

Losartan/Telmisartan (ARBs):
Brand names: Losar, Telma, Telpres
Uses: Hypertension, diabetic nephropathy. Once daily.
Preferred over ACE inhibitors if cough develops.

Atenolol/Metoprolol (Beta-blockers):
Brand names: Aten, Metolar, Betaloc
Uses: Hypertension, angina, heart failure, migraine prevention, hyperthyroidism.
Do not stop abruptly — may cause rebound hypertension/chest pain.

=== VITAMINS AND SUPPLEMENTS ===
Vitamin D3 (Calciferol):
Brand names: D-Rise, Uprise-D3, Calcirol, Arachitol
Uses: Vitamin D deficiency (very common in India despite sun exposure), bone health, immunity.
Dosage: Weekly 60,000 IU for 8-12 weeks, then maintenance monthly.

Vitamin B12 (Methylcobalamin):
Brand names: Methylcobal, Neurobion, Cobadex
Uses: B12 deficiency (common in vegetarians), neuropathy, anaemia.
Note: Plant foods do not contain B12. All strict vegetarians/vegans should supplement.

Iron + Folic Acid:
Brand names: Fefol, Livogen, Dexorange, Autrin
Uses: Iron deficiency anaemia, pregnancy supplementation.
Take with Vitamin C (lemon juice/amla). Avoid tea/coffee within 2 hours.
Government programme: Free IFA tablets under National Health Mission at PHCs.

Zinc:
Brand names: Zincovit, Zinc-P, Zinconia
Uses: Childhood diarrhoea treatment, immunity, wound healing.
ICMR recommends zinc 20mg daily for 14 days with ORS in children with diarrhoea.

=== EMERGENCY MEDICINES ===
Aspirin 300mg: Given immediately if heart attack suspected (chew, don't swallow). Not for fever in children or dengue.

Sorbitrate/Nitroglycerine: Under tongue for angina chest pain. If no relief in 5 minutes — call 112, suspected heart attack.

Salbutamol Inhaler (Ventolin, Asthalin): For acute asthma attack — 2-4 puffs every 20 minutes. If no response — emergency.

Glucagon/Sugar: For hypoglycaemia (low blood sugar) in diabetics — give glucose/sugar water immediately if conscious.
