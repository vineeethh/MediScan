=== WHEN TO GO TO EMERGENCY (CALL 112) ===
CHEST PAIN: Crushing chest pain, pain radiating to left arm/jaw, sweating, shortness of breath — HEART ATTACK. Call 112. Chew aspirin 300mg if not allergic and no dengue/bleeding risk.

STROKE (FAST):
F — Face drooping (one side)
A — Arm weakness (one arm cannot be raised)
S — Speech difficulty (slurred, unable to speak)
T — Time to call 112 immediately
Golden hour for stroke — clot-busting treatment must begin within 4.5 hours.

BREATHING DIFFICULTY: Oxygen saturation below 90%, blue lips (cyanosis), cannot speak full sentences — Emergency.

SEVERE ALLERGIC REACTION (ANAPHYLAXIS): Swelling of throat/tongue, difficulty breathing after insect sting/food/medicine. Epinephrine (Adrenaline) injection needed. Call 112.

SEIZURES: Protect from injury, do not put anything in mouth, turn on side, call 112 if seizure lasts more than 5 minutes or does not stop.

DENGUE WARNING SIGNS: Platelet below 50,000, bleeding, vomiting blood, black stools, severe abdominal pain — Emergency hospitalisation.

DIABETIC EMERGENCY: Low blood sugar — unconscious or confused diabetic — give sugar water if conscious. Call 112 if unconscious. High blood sugar emergency (DKA) — vomiting, deep breathing, fruity breath — Emergency.

SNAKE BITE: Immobilise bitten limb, keep below heart level, remove jewellery, go to hospital immediately. DO NOT apply tourniquet, do NOT suck venom, do NOT make incisions. Anti-snake venom (ASV) available at government hospitals.

DOG BITE/ANIMAL BITE: Wash wound with soap and water for 15 minutes. Get anti-rabies vaccination within 24 hours at nearest government hospital or PHC. Free under National Rabies Control Programme.

=== GOVERNMENT HEALTH FACILITIES ===
Primary Health Centre (PHC): Village/block level. Basic OPD, maternal care, immunisation, free medicines.

Community Health Centre (CHC): Taluk level. 30-bed hospital, specialist doctors.

District Hospital: Full-service hospital, emergency care, surgery.

AIIMS: All India Institute of Medical Sciences — premier centres in Delhi, Hyderabad, Bhopal, Bhubaneswar, Jodhpur, Rishikesh, Raipur, Nagpur, Mangalagiri (Andhra Pradesh), Bibinagar (Telangana).

Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY): Health insurance up to Rs 5 lakh/year for hospitalisation. BPL families and SECC list beneficiaries. Cashless treatment at empanelled hospitals. Helpline: 14555.

=== FIRST AID ===
BURNS: Cool burn under running water for 10-20 minutes. Do NOT apply toothpaste, ice, butter. Cover with clean cloth. Seek medical attention for burns larger than palm size or on face/hands/genitals.

CHOKING: Back blows (5 firm blows between shoulder blades) + abdominal thrusts (Heimlich manoeuvre). For infants — 5 back blows + 5 chest thrusts.

DROWNING: Remove from water. If not breathing — CPR (30 chest compressions + 2 rescue breaths). Call 112.

CPR: 30 chest compressions (hard and fast, 100-120/min) + 2 rescue breaths. Continue until help arrives or person recovers. Hands-only CPR (without rescue breaths) acceptable if untrained.

HEAT STROKE: Temperature above 104°F, confusion, no sweating. Move to cool area. Cool with wet cloth, fan, cold water. Call 112. Do NOT give fluids to unconscious person.

FRACTURE: Immobilise the limb (splint with stiff object). Do not try to realign bone. Cover open wounds. Go to hospital.

=== MENTAL HEALTH CRISIS ===
Signs: Talking about suicide, giving away possessions, extreme hopelessness, self-harm.
Actions: Stay with the person, listen without judgment, remove access to means of harm, contact iCall (9152987821) or take to nearest psychiatric facility.
NIMHANS Bangalore: 080-46110007. Mental health OPD available at all district hospitals under DMHP (District Mental Health Programme).
